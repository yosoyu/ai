# Red Flags Checklist

Critical warning signs to watch for during analysis.

### Management Integrity Issues
- [ ] Management fraud or securities violations
- [ ] Founder/executive legal issues (dishonest debtor, lawsuits)
- [ ] Failed privatization at lowball price
- [ ] History of misleading shareholders
- [ ] Accounting irregularities or restatements

### Governance Failures
- [ ] Excessive related party transactions
- [ ] Family control with poor minority protection
- [ ] Board lacking independence
- [ ] Excessive executive compensation
- [ ] No succession planning

### Financial Distress
- [ ] Current ratio <1.0
- [ ] Debt covenant violations
- [ ] Going concern warnings
- [ ] Auditor concerns or resignation
- [ ] Bankruptcy risk

## âš ï¸ Major Warning Signs (Investigate Further)

### Business Model Concerns
- [ ] Declining market share
- [ ] New competitive threats
- [ ] Technology disruption
- [ ] Regulatory headwinds
- [ ] Loss of key customers

### Financial Warning Signs
- [ ] ROE declining over multiple years
- [ ] Margin compression
- [ ] Revenue growing but profits declining
- [ ] Negative free cash flow (excluding growth capex)
- [ ] Working capital deterioration

### Balance Sheet Concerns
- [ ] Rising debt levels
- [ ] Short-term debt > long-term debt
- [ ] Off-balance sheet liabilities
- [ ] Large goodwill relative to assets
- [ ] Inventory buildup

### Cash Flow Issues
- [ ] Operating cash flow < net income consistently
- [ ] Capex consistently > depreciation (without growth)
- [ ] Dividend not covered by free cash flow
- [ ] Share issuance diluting shareholders

---

## ðŸŸ¡ Moderate Warning Signs (Monitor)

### Dividend Concerns
- [ ] Payout ratio >80% (or >100%)
- [ ] Dividend cut history
- [ ] Dividend not covered by earnings
- [ ] Borrowing to pay dividend

### Concentration Risk
- [ ] Single customer >20% of revenue
- [ ] Single supplier critical to operations
- [ ] Single product >80% of revenue
- [ ] Geographic concentration in risky markets

### Valuation Concerns
- [ ] P/E >50x without exceptional growth
- [ ] Trading far above historical average
- [ ] Insider selling
- [ ] Secondary offerings

### Market Signals
- [ ] Short interest increasing
- [ ] Analyst downgrades
- [ ] Institutional selling
- [ ] Low liquidity
